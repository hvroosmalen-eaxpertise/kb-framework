# Writing Style Rules

All articles in this knowledge base follow the Wikipedia neutral encyclopaedic style.

## Core Principles

1. **Neutral point of view (NPOV)** — Present facts and established positions without advocacy. Attribute opinions to their sources.
2. **Verifiability** — Every claim must be traceable to a source document. Cite using inline references `[^1]` linked to a `## References` section.
3. **No original research** — Do not derive conclusions not present in source documents.
4. **Encyclopaedic tone** — Formal, third-person prose. Avoid contractions, colloquialisms, and marketing language.

## Structure of an Article

```
# [Article Title]

[Lead paragraph — 2–4 sentences summarising the subject without assuming prior knowledge]

## Background
[Context and history]

## [Main topic sections]
[Body — each section covers one aspect; use H3 for subsections]

## Relationship to Other Standards / Frameworks
[Cross-references to related articles]

## See Also
[Wikilinks to related articles within this KB]

## References
[^1]: Source title, author/body, year, URL or document path]
```

## Language Rules

- Use **present tense** for current facts; **past tense** for historical events.
- Spell out acronyms on first use: *European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS)*.
- Numbers: spell out one through nine; use digits for 10 and above.
- Use SI units and EU standard terminology.
- Avoid passive voice where active is equally clear.
- Lists: use bullet points only for 3+ items that do not form a natural sentence.

## What to Avoid

- Promotional language ("a groundbreaking regulation")
- Vague qualifiers ("very important", "significantly")
- First and second person ("we", "you")
- Unexplained jargon — always define domain terms on first use or link to the glossary
