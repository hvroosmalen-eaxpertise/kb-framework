# Cross-Referencing Rules

Cross-references connect related articles within and across knowledge bases.

## Within an Article

Use wikilinks in the form `[[Article Title]]` for internal references.
MkDocs renders these as relative links — ensure the target file exists.

```markdown
The concept of [[Double Materiality]] is central to [[CSRD]] compliance
and is operationalised through the [[ESRS 1]] general requirements.
```

## See Also Section

Every article must have a `## See Also` section with at least two links.

## Cross-Reference Matrix

The file `docs/cross-reference-matrix.md` is auto-generated by `query.py`.
It maps every standard/directive article to related articles across domains.

Format:
```markdown
| Article | Related ESRS | Related SDG | Related GRI |
|---|---|---|---|
| CSRD Overview | ESRS 1, ESRS 2 | SDG-13, SDG-17 | GRI 2 |
```

## Rules

1. Never link to a non-existent article — create a stub first.
2. A stub contains only frontmatter and a one-line definition.
3. Links to external sources go in `## References`, not inline.
4. When two articles from different domains define the same concept differently, note this explicitly in both articles under a `## Terminology Note` subsection.
